Beyond the work

The PM outside the PRD.

"I think in systems everywhere — not just in products. Whether it's a broken ops workflow, an obscure piece of history, or how a city moves at rush hour. The curiosity never stops."

Passionate quizzer

Ex-member of the quiz club at IIM Kashipur. Organised Rumble in the Jungle — a pan-India quiz with 2,600+ participants. Quizzing taught me how to find connections between things that seem unrelated. A skill that shows up in product work every day.

History buff

Fascinated by how things came to be — empires, technologies, ideas. History is the original case study library. Every pattern in modern business has a precedent somewhere in the past, if you look hard enough.

Storyteller

Numbers without narrative don't land. I believe the best PMs are translators — turning complexity into something a stakeholder can act on, and an engineer can build. The story is part of the product.

Believer in science

Mechanical engineer by training. Science taught me to question assumptions, run experiments, and respect data. The same mindset that makes a good researcher makes a good PM — curious, rigorous, and willing to be proven wrong.
